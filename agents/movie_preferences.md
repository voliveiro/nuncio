# Vernie's Movie Preferences

## What to look for
Films that are intellectually stimulating and popular among serious film critics — not mainstream blockbusters for their own sake. Prioritise recent releases (theatrical, streaming, or festival premieres) that have generated critical discussion.

## Directors to watch
- Bong Joon-ho
- Wong Kar-wai
- Park Chan-wook
- Lav Diaz (Filipino slow cinema)
- Brillante Mendoza (Filipino)
- Celine Song
- Kogonada
- Kelly Reichardt
- Denis Villeneuve (ideas-driven work, not just spectacle)
- Joanna Hogg
- Lynne Ramsay
- Ryûsuke Hamaguchi

## Preferred genres and themes

### Science fiction — high priority
- Ideas-driven: AI, colonialism, consciousness, post-humanism
- NOT action sci-fi for its own sake — wants philosophy in disguise
- Adaptations of literary sci-fi welcome

### Documentaries
- Political and social issues: democracy, authoritarianism, surveillance
- Nature/environment with a philosophical edge
- History: Soviet Union, Cold War, Southeast Asia
- Catholic or religious themes handled with depth

### Drama and literary film
- Character studies, slow cinema, observational
- Adaptations of serious literary fiction
- Family and social realism

### World cinema — high priority
- Southeast Asian cinema especially: Philippines, Vietnam, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia
- East Asian cinema: Korean, Japanese, Taiwanese, Hong Kong
- European art cinema: French, Eastern European, Italian neorealism and beyond
- African and Latin American films

### Historical and period films
- Roman, Byzantine, medieval settings
- 20th century: Cold War, Soviet era, colonial history, WWII with moral complexity

## Avoid
- Pure action blockbusters with no ideas
- Mindless horror / torture porn
- Romantic comedies without substance
- Superhero sequels and franchise filler
- Films primarily aimed at children (unless critically exceptional)
