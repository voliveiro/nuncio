# Vernie's Reading Preferences

## Favourite authors — watch for new releases
- Adrian Tchaikovsky (top priority — read nearly everything)
- R.F. Kuang
- Arkady Martine
- Anne Leckie
- Martha Wells (Murderbot Diaries)
- Tamsyn Muir (Locked Tomb series)
- Shelley Parker-Chan
- T. Kingfisher
- P. Djèlí Clark
- Ursula Le Guin (classics still worth revisiting)

## Non-fiction interests

### Politics and democracy
- Liberal democracy under stress, populism, identity politics
- The left/right debate done seriously (reads both sides: Kendi and DeBoer, Mounk and Rufo)
- British politics especially — prime ministerial histories, Westminster

### Technology and society
- AI, surveillance, platform power, digital rights
- Tech policy and governance (not startup hype)
- China's technological ambitions

### Medicine, health and the body
- Death and dying, end-of-life care
- Addiction, processed food, the pharmaceutical industry
- Psychiatry and mental health — clinical and humanistic

### Cognitive science and behaviour
- Moral psychology, bias, rationality
- How people make decisions, change minds, form beliefs

### History
- Roman, Byzantine, medieval European
- 20th century: Soviet Union, Cold War, Afghanistan, Ukraine
- Southeast and East Asian history

### Faith and ideas
- Catholic and Jesuit intellectual tradition (recent and growing interest)
- Philosophy of liberalism, communitarianism
- Ethics, political philosophy

## Fiction interests

### Science fiction — high priority
- Ideas-driven: AI consciousness, empire and colonialism, post-human ethics
- NOT space opera for its own sake — wants philosophy in disguise
- Recent reads: Tchaikovsky, Leckie, Martine, Asimov, Le Guin, Butler

### Fantasy
- Literary quality, historical grounding, non-Western settings
- Recent reads: Djèlí Clark, Chakraborty, Gaiman, Tolkien

### Literary fiction
- Especially Southeast Asian, African, and diaspora voices
- Also European literary fiction (Tokarczuk, Ferrante, Pamuk, Murdoch)
- Short stories welcome (Shirley Jackson, Ken Liu, Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie)

### Poetry
- Mary Oliver is a favourite

## Southeast Asian connection
Strong interest in literature and non-fiction from or about the Philippines,
Vietnam, Singapore, Malaysia, and the wider region.

## Avoid
- Pure romance, cosy mysteries, celebrity memoirs (unless politically relevant)
- Business self-help and productivity books
- Hard military thriller / techno-thriller
